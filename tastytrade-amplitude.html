The Gap

Where insight
goes to wait

01
Acquisition answers are stuck behind data stitching

Which channels drive quality customers? What's the expected LTV by source? How does pre-acquisition web behavior connect to post-acquisition outcomes? These are fundamental questions — and answering any one of them requires joining data from several disconnected platforms. One question, multiple pipelines, days of lead time before analysis can even start.

"Getting the multitude of systems to talk to each other... answering a single acquisition question often requires stitching together data from several disconnected platforms." — tastytrade
02
Marketing and product have no shared view of the funnel

Marketing optimizes for acquisition. Product optimizes for activation. But the behaviors that connect the two — what a user did on the site before signing up, which features they touched first, how long until first trade — live in separate ecosystems. You can't connect brand to performance when they live in different tools with different metrics and different owners.

03
New product questions require engineering before insight

When a product question comes up that doesn't have existing tracking, the answer requires engineering to instrument first — adding lead time before data collection even begins. By the time insight is available, the decision has already been made on gut feel. A self-service behavioral analytics layer breaks this dependency so teams can move from question to answer without a sprint cycle in between.

"New instrumentation requires engineering, which adds lead time before we can even start collecting data." — tastytrade
04
Rockerbox is hitting its ceiling on attribution

Multi-touch attribution that ends at acquisition misses the most important part of the story for tastytrade: what happens from sign-up to funded account to first trade. The channel that drives account opens isn't always the one that drives traders who stay. Without behavioral data downstream, you're optimizing for the wrong conversion event.

Why Now

The compounding cost of waiting

Three things are working against tastytrade's growth right now. Each one gets harder to fix the longer it runs.

Every week without behavioral data is a week of acquisition spend flying blind

tastytrade is spending on channels today without knowing which ones produce traders who fund and place a first trade within the first week. That's the cohort that matters. Until behavioral data connects acquisition to activation, every channel decision is a bet on proxies — clicks, signups — rather than the outcomes that actually predict LTV.

Competitors who understand the account-to-trade journey will optimize it faster

The self-directed investing market is competitive. The platforms that win are the ones that reduce friction between signup and first trade most effectively — and they do it by running experiments on every step of that journey. You can't experiment on what you can't measure. The gap between tastytrade's current visibility and what's possible widens every quarter.

Instrumentation debt compounds — the right time to establish a behavioral layer is before scale forces you to

As tastytrade grows, the number of teams asking behavioral questions grows with it. The Director of Internal Tools and Manager of Data Analytics & BI joining this conversation signals that demand for self-serve insight is already broader than product. Building the right foundation now means every team can access behavioral data — not just the ones with engineering resources to instrument it themselves.
